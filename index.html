About Me

I am a PhD graduate who is currently working at the Ministry of Justice.

I am a Behavioural Scientist within a User Experience Team at the Ministry of Justice.

I have recently been awarded a PhD in Psychology from the University of Liverpool. My PhD explored jurors' memory and decsion making. Throughout my PhD, I worked as a Statistics Teacher at the university.

I have a BA in Psychology & Criminology, and MSc in Investigative & Forensic Psychology. I previously worked as a modelling analyst at HM Revenue & Customs.

In my free time, I volunteer, travel and read.
